# Building Taste With AI

AI commoditized execution. Curation is now scarce. Every first output from AI looks like an app store landing page, a Medium post, or a hero section template. It is not terrible, but it is predictable. Predictable is the enemy of taste.

## The problem: statistical taste

AI trained on massive datasets produces culturally averaged aesthetics. It predicts what usually works, reproduces rewarded patterns, and compresses thousands of references into one safe-good output. Safe-good is never tasteful.

Before AI, mediocre work took effort. Effort created noise you could confuse with quality. AI removed that noise. Now you can generate ten versions of the same slop in ten minutes, and the gap between what AI produces and what you actually want is where your taste lives.

The average is the new mediocre. Everyone can ship a 7/10. The only way to stand out is judgment, selection, and specificity.

## The core loop: generate, then destroy

1. Produce 10 to 20 versions of anything.
2. Do not use them to pick the best one.
3. Use them to develop a rejection vocabulary.

The muscle you are training: "This fails because X." X must be structural, not cosmetic. Do that enough times and you internalize a framework you could not have articulated beforehand.

## The specificity test

Every time AI generates a version, ask: could this have been written about anything else?

If you could swap the subject out and nothing changes, it has no taste. Taste is irreducibly contextual. Rick Rubin's production on Californication sounds like nothing else because it was made for those songs, at that moment, by that group of people.

## Find the canon, then ignore the canon

Use AI to map the best work in any field across the last 100 or 200 years. Read it. Watch it. Absorb it. Use deep research not to copy it, but to understand why it worked there. Curiosity is the only differentiator between average taste and curated taste.

## Make with stakes

Taste that never risks anything is passive. Publish the essay. Ship the product. Present the deck. The feedback loop of real output is irreplaceable. AI accelerates everything except feedback. The prerequisite for taste is knowing what rules you are willing to break, and you cannot break rules you do not know.

## Resistance as signal

When something AI produces bothers you and you cannot say why, do not move on. Sit with that friction. That is the raw material. Most people scroll past it. The person who stops and interrogates what is wrong is doing the actual work. Over time, those moments become your aesthetic immune system.

## AI will not replace taste with perspective

AI will eventually have better taste than most humans at most things, because taste is trainable on human output. What AI cannot replace is taste with a perspective. Rubin does not just know what sounds good. He knows what sounds good given what he believes music is for. That belief is not derivable from a dataset. It comes from living, choosing, and losing.

## The taste triad

- **Curiosity.** Not collecting inspiration. Interrogating it.
- **Judgment.** Selection and rejection at the same time.
- **Standards.** The lines you will not cross. If you do not have them, AI will supply culturally averaged ones for you.

## 10-minute daily practice

1. Pick one output: a paragraph, a landing page, a tweet.
2. Generate 10 versions with AI.
3. Write one critique line for each using the phrase "fails because..."
4. Rewrite one version under a single constraint (no adjectives, no gradients, one idea).
5. Publish it.

That loop builds taste faster than consuming more inspiration.

## The rule

Do not let hard process excuse bad results. AI made the process easy. Now taste is the only thing left that matters.
